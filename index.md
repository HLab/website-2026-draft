<div class="section">
  <h2>About</h2>
  <p>Welcome to the Honey Lab in the <a href="https://pbs.jhu.edu/">Department of Psychological &amp; Brain Sciences</a> at <a href="https://www.jhu.edu/">Johns Hopkins University</a>. We study how the brain makes sense of the world in real time — transforming continuous experience into structured memories, extracting meaning from language and narrative, and spontaneously recalling the past. We combine neuroimaging, behavioral experiments, and machine learning to understand these processes.</p>
</div>

<div class="section">

  <p>Our research investigates spontaneous and automatic memory processes and their role in cognitive architectures, and explores open-ended cognition in both humans and machines. We use naturalistic paradigms — speech, narrative, film — to study how the brain segments experience, forms and retrieves memories, and builds meaning over time.</p>
