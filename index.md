<div class="section">
  <h2>About</h2>
  <p>Welcome to the Honey Lab at <a href="https://www.jhu.edu/">Johns Hopkins University</a>. We study how the brain makes sense of the world in real time, and the kinds of cognitive architectures that make this possible. At Johns Hopkins, our home is in the <a href="https://pbs.jhu.edu/">Department of Psychological &amp; Brain Sciences</a>, and we are affiliated with the <a href="https://neuroscience.jhu.edu/">Department of Neuroscience</a> and the <a href="https://ai.jhu.edu/">Data Science and AI Institute</a>.</p>
</div>

  <p>Our goal is to understand how memory processes, especially spontaneous and automatic memory processes, shape thinking in humans and in machines. By studying brain responses to narratives, film, and open-ended thinking tasks, we have mapped how we segment thoughts and experiences, maintain mental context, and construct meaning over time.</p>
